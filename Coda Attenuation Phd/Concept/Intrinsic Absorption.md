Assumption --\> intrinsic adsorption is weakly varying
- Spatial variations in coda amplitude --\> scattering heterogeneity
- CN cancels adsorption effects

Yoshimoto et al 2006
Examined S coda energy spanning a volcanic front (VF)
![image1](167b3e9777ad40ab9ad232c535ec7358.png)
East VF --\> coda energy uniform
West VF --\> coda energy decreases exponentially with distance
Decay stronger at higher frequencies

In this case coda energy variations are controlled by adsorption, not scattering
Problem in CN --\> assumes **spatially uniform coda energy** at late lapse times
In this case coda energy is not uniform

Normal condition assumption of the CN model
1.  Random, homogenous distribution of scatterers
2.  Isotropic scattering
3.  Weak intrinsic adsorption
4.  Late lapse time (multiple scattering regime)
5.  No site trapping or guided waves
