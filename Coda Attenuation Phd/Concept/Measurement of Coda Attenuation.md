Equation has 2 parts:

    - Geometrical spreading/scattering regime
    - n-values depend on dominant type:
      - Single scattering n=2
      - Diffusion n = 3/2
2.  ![image3](a8cdbbb9448a4be69a2dd329a1223170.png)
Independent of source, station, distance
Depends only on center frequency
Qc estimation in practice
1.  Band-pass filter seismogram (octave-width, eg 2-4, 4-8, 8-16)
2.  Compute coda envelope
3.  Choose time window (after 2ts)
4.  ![image4](054a1eb1df51496db5ada4cda49d4ae3.png)
5.  Take log value
6.  Fit a straight line vs lapse time
7.  Slope --\> Qc
Frequency bands matter
Too narrow --\> envelope fluctuates
Too wide --\> mixes different attenuation behavior
Best compromise -\> use octave band

Common Qc variation
High Qc --\> stable region, cold dry lithosphere
Lowe Qc --\> active tectonic, mountain belts, volcanic, fluid saturated

Qc values often similar with Qs (intrinsic)
