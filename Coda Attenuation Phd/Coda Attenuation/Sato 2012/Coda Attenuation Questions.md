Margerin 2017 paper --\> equipartition
The strong asymmetry observed in the “early” coda progressively vanishes as the waves undergo more and more scattering and surface reflections in the sample
Lack temporal symmetry or asymmetry --\> imperfect equipartition --\> early coda lapse time
The level of scattering in the crust in Alaska is too weak to reach equipartition before the signal enters the noise level.
**can small earthquake or aftershock coda reach equipartition?**

Equipartition can only be reached if there is enough level of scattering,
that is why in tw = 2ts case, the coda vs raypath length will not have an independent relation, because it will contain very local earthquakes with short coda window.
In these earthquakes, the scattering ellipse is small --\> scattering level is low --\> imperfect equipartition --\> no independent relation
